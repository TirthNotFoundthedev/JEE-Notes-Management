01.5 General Characteristics

Why are d-Orbitals Special?

The d-orbitals of the transition elements protrude to the periphery of an atom more than the other orbitals (i.e., s and p), hence, they are more influenced by the surroundings as well as affect the atoms or molecules surrounding them. In some respects, ions of a given dn configuration (n = 1-9) have similar magnetic and electronic properties.

With partly filled d orbitals these elements exhibit certain characteristic properties such as display of a variety of oxidation states, formation of coloured ions and entering into complex formation with a variety of ligands. The transition metals and their compounds also exhibit catalytic property and paramagnetic behaviour. All these characteristics have been discussed in detail later in this Unit.

There are greater similarities in the properties of the transition elements of a horizontal row in contrast to the non-transition elements. However, some group similarities also exist. We shall first study the general characteristics and their trends in the horizontal rows (particularly 3d row) and then consider some group similarities.

05. Inner Transition (f-Block)

Feature Lanthanoids (4f) Actinoids (5f)
General Config [Xe] 4f1-14 5d0-1 6s2 [Rn] 5f1-14 6d0-1 7s2
Oxidation States Most stable: +3.
Ce(+4) is stable (noble gas config). Eu(+2) is stable (half-filled).		 Variable (+3, +4, +5, +6, +7).
Reason: Comparable energies of 5f, 6d, 7s.
Contraction Lanthanoid Contraction: Poor shielding of 4f electrons. Size decreases regularly. Actinoid Contraction: Greater than Lanthanoid contraction because 5f shielding is even poorer than 4f.
Reactivity Less reactive (like Al/Ca). Highly reactive (especially when finely divided). Radioactive.
Uses Mischmetall (95% Ln, 5% Fe) - used in bullets/lighters. Nuclear fuel (Th, U, Pu).

Key Takeaway: Lanthanoid Contraction

The steady decrease in atomic size along the lanthanoid series.
Consequence: The elements of the 2nd and 3rd transition series (e.g., Zr and Hf, Nb and Ta) have almost identical radii and properties, making separation difficult.
