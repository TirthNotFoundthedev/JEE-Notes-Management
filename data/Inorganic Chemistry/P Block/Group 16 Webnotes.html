1 Trends: The Oxygen Anomaly

Electron Gain Enthalpy (ΔegH)

Oxygen has LESS negative ΔegH than Sulphur.

Why? Small size of Oxygen causes inter-electronic repulsion in the compact 2p subshell.

Hydride Boiling Points

Why is water liquid but H₂S a gas?

Order: H₂S < H₂Se < H₂Te < H₂O
(Massive H-Bonding in Water)

The Solid State: Sulphur's Crown

  • Oxygen (O₂): Stable multiple bonds (pπ-pπ). Gas.
  • Sulphur (S₈): No pπ-pπ bonds. Forms S-S single bonds (Catenation). Solid.

Rhombic vs Monoclinic

Transition Temp: 369K

Below 369K: Rhombic (α) stable.

Above 369K: Monoclinic (β) stable.

3 The "Gotcha" Zone

Is S₂ Paramagnetic?

YES. Like O₂, S₂ (in vapor phase) has unpaired electrons in antibonding π* orbitals.

Usually S exists as S₈ (Diamagnetic), but at high temp (>1000K), S₂ dominates.

Why is SF₆ so Inert?

Ideally, S has +6 oxidation state (high attraction), but it doesn't react.

Reason: Steric Hindrance. The 6 Fluorine atoms protect the Sulphur atom from attack.
