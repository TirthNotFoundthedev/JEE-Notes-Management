1 Trends & Anomalies

Atomic Radius Anomaly

Expected to increase down the group, but Gallium is smaller than Aluminum.

Why? Poor shielding of d-electrons in Gallium (Transition Contraction) pulls the outer shell closer.

Ionization Enthalpy (IE₁)

Does NOT decrease regularly. It follows a "W" shape pattern.

Order: B > Tl > Ga > Al > In
*Tl is very high due to Lanthanoid Contraction (4f shielding).

Oxidation States & Inert Pair Effect

  • Common States: +3 and +1.
  • Stability of +1: Increases down the group.
  • Stability of +3: Decreases down the group.
Stability Order
Al³⁺ > Ga³⁺ > In³⁺ > Tl³⁺
Tl⁺ > In⁺ > Ga⁺ > Al⁺

Consequences:

  • ➤ TlCl₃ is unstable compared to TlCl.
  • ➤ Tl³⁺ is a strong Oxidizing Agent (wants to become Tl⁺).
  • ➤ Al⁺ is a strong Reducing Agent (wants to become Al³⁺).
